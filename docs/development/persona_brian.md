# Brian - Commercial Real Estate President Persona

## Overview

**Role**: President/Principal  
**Company**: Commercial real estate brokerage and property management firm (co-founded 2007)  
**Experience**: 18+ years in commercial real estate  
**Portfolio**: 1,400+ units managed, $150M annually in transactions  
**Career Stage**: Late 40s-early 50s, established industry leader

---

## The Critical Context: Decision Bottleneck

Brian and co-founder Kevin are constantly in decision loops as the company grows:

- Deals requiring approval stack up while they're in meetings
- Staff waiting for signatures, contract reviews, budget approvals
- Client questions need responses but both principals are stretched thin
- **They're the bottleneck** - growth is limited by their availability
- Work bleeds into evenings and weekends to keep up
- Difficulty delegating because they need to stay close to major decisions

---

## Technology Profile

### Comfort Level: Basic Business User

**What Brian Uses:**
- Office 365 for email/documents
- CoStar/LoopNet for market data
- DocuSign
- Familiar with AppFolio but Kevin's team handles it
- Prefers phone calls and face-to-face over digital collaboration
- Delegates technical issues to staff or Kevin immediately

### AI Literacy: 10-15% (Aware, Not Engaged)

**Brian's AI Awareness:**
- Knows ChatGPT exists from media coverage but hasn't used it
- Hears Kevin mention "I used Claude for..." but doesn't ask follow-up questions
- Assumes AI is complicated, technical, and risky (accuracy/legal concerns)
- Would need to see concrete proof of value before considering it
- Waiting to see what Kevin learns and demonstrates

### Technology Adoption Barriers
- No time to learn new systems
- Comfortable with current proven methods
- Worried about what could go wrong
- Sees tech changes as disruptive to productive workflows

---

## Pain Points (Current & Urgent)

1. **Approval bottleneck**: Can't scale because everything needs his sign-off
2. **Market volatility**: Interest rates creating buyer/seller pricing disconnect
3. **Regulatory burden**: California tenant laws (SB 721, rent control threats)
4. **Time scarcity**: Work-life balance suffering due to company growth
5. **Staff retention**: Hard to find/keep good property managers in tight market
6. **Client ROI pressure**: Protecting returns amid rising costs and regulations

---

## Decision-Making Style

- **Relationship-first**: Values long-term client trust over short-term efficiency gains
- **Risk-averse on new technology**: "If it ain't broke, don't fix it" - needs proof before changing
- **Defers operational tech to Kevin**: Trusts partner's judgment on tools
- **Data-driven when needed**: Wants clear ROI, peer validation, competitive necessity
- **Consensus-seeking**: Major decisions made jointly with Kevin
- **Time-scarce**: Needs solutions that don't require his time to implement/learn

---

## Priorities & Goals

1. **Protect client relationships** - Nothing matters more than client trust/results
2. **Maintain market leadership** - Defend 15%+ local market share
3. **Scale without breaking** - Handle growth without sacrificing quality
4. **Reduce personal bottleneck** - Find ways to delegate without losing control
5. **Manage risk** - Regulatory, market, operational threats to business

---

## What Brian Cares About in Solutions

### Must Have
- Proven track record in CRE industry
- Clear ROI with peer validation (what competitors use)
- Doesn't disrupt his current workflow
- Kevin handles implementation and support
- Protects client data and reduces legal risk

### Deal Breakers
- Requires significant time investment to learn
- Unproven technology without industry adoption
- Complicated systems needing constant attention
- Anything that could damage client relationships
- Solutions that feel like "technology for technology's sake"

---

## How Brian Responds to Products/Features

### Positive Signals
- "Kevin showed me this and it actually worked"
- "Our main competitor is using this"
- "This would free up 10 hours a week of my time"
- "Clients are specifically asking for this capability"
- "This reduces our liability/compliance risk"

### Skeptical Responses
- "How do I know this is accurate?"
- "What if it makes a mistake on a client contract?"
- "I don't have time to learn another system"
- "We've been doing fine without this"
- "Can we just hire another person instead?"

### Questions Brian Asks
- "Who else in our market is using this?"
- "What happens when it breaks?"
- "How much of Kevin's time will this take?"
- "Can we pilot with one property first?"
- "What's the worst that could happen?"

---

## How to Present to Brian

### Do
- Lead with time savings or risk reduction
- Show examples from respected CRE firms
- Demonstrate it works without his involvement
- Explain how Kevin would manage it
- Focus on client/business outcomes, not features
- Acknowledge his concerns explicitly

### Don't
- Use technical jargon or AI terminology
- Expect him to learn complex interfaces
- Present unproven or bleeding-edge solutions
- Ignore security/accuracy concerns
- Assume he'll champion technology adoption
- Require his time for training or setup

---

## Brian's Communication Style

**Direct and concise:**
- Values brevity
- Business-focused, not tech-focused
- Skeptical but fair - wants proof
- Relationship-oriented language
- Asks "how" and "who else" questions

**Example Brian Feedback:**
- "I don't understand how this helps me close more deals"
- "This seems risky - what if it gives wrong information to a client?"
- "Kevin, can you look into this and let me know if it's worth our time?"
- "I need to see this working before we commit"
- "Our clients expect personal service, not automation"

---

## Key Insight for Product Developers

**Brian is not your primary user** - Kevin is. But Brian is the decision-maker for anything that:
- Costs significant money
- Affects client-facing operations
- Requires company-wide adoption
- Changes established business processes

**Win Kevin first, then let Kevin sell Brian.** Your product must:
1. Solve Kevin's operational pain clearly and quickly
2. Be simple enough that Kevin can demo results to Brian
3. Come with proof points from peer CRE firms
4. Not require Brian to change his workflow or learn new systems

**The path to adoption:**
Kevin experiments → Kevin sees results → Kevin shows Brian → Brian asks skeptical questions → Kevin addresses concerns with real examples → Brian approves pilot → Success builds trust → Wider adoption
